Today we will be taking about primate conservation.

The reasons we need to be worried about primate conservation are as follows:

  1. Primates serve as a key functions for the environment they inhabit. This includes a key part in regeneration of the rain forest.
  2. Primates provide a unique insight into human evolution.
  3. Primates are an umbrella species.This means that in order to stop their extinction the entire rain forest must also be conserved. Therefore their conservation benefits the entire ecosystem.
  4. 10 percent of the worlds species live in the rain forest.
  5. Most importantly the rain forest produces a vast amount of oxygen for our planet.

    6. It is also important to note that the rain forest floor stores a vast amount of carbon. This reduces the amount of carbon going into our atmosphere. However, when the forest is burned down this releases that carbon storage into the atmosphere.

Why is the rain forest being cut down?

Most of the rain forest are being cut down in order to make more space for production. Due to the growing population and the growing demand for resources space needs to be cleared. The forests are being cut down for palm plantations, sugar cane, cattle, etc. However, the rain forest floor is actually nutrient poor. This means that when it is used for production it is quickly depleted of those vital nutrients needed for crops or grass. Therefore, more forest is needed to cut down in order to continue production and leave the old land to turn to a desert.
